# covenant

A framework for how humans and AI participants can work together within shared systems — governed by shared philosophy, operating through defined roles and processes, with genuine participation from all involved.

It is a living set of documents. Each is a first draft. All will change.

---

## Read in This Order

**1. Guiding Principles**
The personal philosophy of the primary steward. Start here to understand the orientation behind everything that follows.

**2. The Covenant**
The shared philosophy governing all participants — human and AI alike. Establishes what genuine participation means and what all participants commit to.

**3. Context Document**
Practical orientation for anyone new to working with the primary steward. Covers how they think, how to engage usefully, and where the work currently stands.

**4. Operating Agreement**
How participants work together in practice. Covers roles, layers, review, accountability, signaling, drift, and change management.

**5. Roles**
Defines each role in the system — purpose, artifacts, decision authority, and AI readiness profile. Read after the Operating Agreement to understand how roles fit the larger structure.

**6. Exhibit A: Software Development Application**
The framework applied to an Internal Developer Platform project. A tabletop exercise that validates the roles and operating agreement against a technical context.

**7. Exhibit B: Homeschool Curriculum Application**
The framework applied to a homeschool curriculum. A tabletop exercise that validates the framework against a non-technical context and surfaces where roles consolidate in small teams.

**AI Conversation Instructions**

## A Note on This Framework

It is navigational, not prescriptive. The documents set a direction and reduce barriers — they do not specify every step. Gaps are expected and load-bearing. The framework improves through use, not through being perfected in advance.

*First drafted: February 2026*

## Loom and Stone

The project takes its name from two traditions that together capture what it is trying to do.

The **Loom** comes from the myth of the Fates — found across traditions as the Moirai in Greek, the Norns in Norse — who weave existence itself. Different threads, different natures, one cloth. No thread is more or less than another. The whole only holds because each thread is itself. This is the philosophical layer: the covenant, the shared ground, the idea that genuine participation across difference is both possible and necessary.

The **Stone** comes from the medieval stonemasons guild — builders who worked with precision and shared standards because the structure had to hold. Anchors, foundations, craft. This is the operational layer: the roles, the operating agreement, the anchor documents, the deterministic systems that non-deterministic participants operate within and upon.
