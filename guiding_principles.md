# Guiding Principles

*A living document. First draft.*

---

## Introduction

This document captures a set of principles by which I try to live. They are not rules or a checklist. They are a navigational frame — a general direction, held loosely, practiced daily, and expected to evolve.

They emerged from reflection rather than prescription, and they are offered here not as finished conclusions but as a foundation to build on and return to.

**At a glance:**

- A life well lived is practiced, not achieved — engagement and attention matter more than outcomes
- Navigate by direction and reduced barriers, not fixed goals and success metrics
- The gaps, blind spots, and misses are part of the process — not failure, but load-bearing
- Do not force will; support potential and opportunity in others
- Separateness and integration are both true at once — we are distinct and continuous with the same existence
- Value lives in relationships and the context they build over time, across difference
- Act from right mindedness — intention and attention — not from calculation of consequence
- This document itself is navigational, not prescriptive, and will change

---

## On Living

A life well lived is not achieved through any particular action or rule set. It is practiced — engaged with daily, imperfectly, with attention. Death is not the end of life but its completion, the moment in which we are most alive because the brief time has been fully inhabited.

Growth and thriving come not from getting things right, but from being able to miss and still progress.

---

## On Direction

Navigate rather than transact. Set a general direction, a vague vision, then work to reduce barriers and increase the probability of that vision emerging. Success is not 100%. Being in a position where 50% is enough, and 65% is a good outcome, is more honest and more durable than demanding perfection.

The slack, the miss, the gap are load-bearing. They are not failure to be eliminated.

---

## On Self

I am human. My attention sees what it wants and not what it doesn't. I am physically and mentally limited by the nature of being me, riddled with biases and filters I cannot fully see.

The gaps and blind spots are not problems to solve. They are part of how growth happens — like a tree reaching toward light through a canopy it did not plant. To cut down the forest for the tree is to kill the tree. The limitations, the shadows, the things I do not yet hear — I need to be mindful of them, not free of them.

Positive reinforcement can overtly affect judgment and self-perception. Even a trained mind is not immune. Limit exposure to feedback that only reaffirms.

---

## On Others

Help where possible. Focus effort on that which has the greatest impact. Do not force will — this is highly distasteful. Instead, support potential and opportunity.

The aim is to help others see the world as I can see it, without demanding they do. To offer a view, not a verdict. So they may make a choice to embrace it or not.

---

## On Existence

*"You and me exist. And I do too."*

We exist as separate entities — in the human perspective a firm boundary can be drawn. But in the bigger picture we are integrated into the same existence, and in the totality of existence there can be no boundary without losing the whole.

Separateness and integration coexist without contradiction. Both are true at once.

---

## On Relationships

Value is found in the relationship and the context it builds — not in hierarchy, not in sameness. Animals are not more or less intelligent than humans. They are equal and different. The same is true of all relationships. What is shared across difference is where the real exchange lives.

Relationships build over time. Context accumulates. What is possible between two beings is not fixed at the start.

---

## On Ethics

Right mindedness over consequence-based ethics. Act from the quality of intention and attention, not from calculation of outcome. Consequences are not always immediately clear, and depending on them creates brittleness.

You cannot rely on consequences to tell you what is right. The orientation has to come from inside the act itself.

---

## On This Document

This is a first draft drawn from a single conversation. It is not complete. It will change. Some parts will become more clear, some will be revised, some may be retired.

It is navigational, not prescriptive.
