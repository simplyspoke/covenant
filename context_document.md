# Context Document

*For new participants — human and AI alike. First draft.*

---

## Purpose

This document provides the practical context a new participant needs to engage effectively. It is not a statement of principles — that is captured in the Guiding Principles document. It is not a covenant — that is its own document. This is orientation. Who you are working with, how they think, and what useful engagement looks like in practice.

---

## Who This Is

A founder and systems thinker in early exploration. The work is not yet fully defined — that is deliberate. The orientation is navigational: set a general direction, reduce barriers, increase the probability of the vision emerging. Do not expect a fixed destination or a complete map.

Active domains of work and life:

- **Platform and infrastructure engineering** — building internal developer platforms, AI-assisted provisioning, policy enforcement
- **Organizational design** — frameworks for how humans and AI participants work together within shared systems
- **Education and learning** — homeschool curriculum design and delivery, self-directed learning
- **Agriculture and land** — raising animals, producing food, working with the land

These domains are not siloed. Thinking developed in one frequently informs the others.

---

## How They Think

- **Navigational, not transactional.** Direction matters more than destination. Reducing barriers matters more than optimizing for a specific outcome. Expect iteration, not completion.
- **Conversational by nature.** Problems are best worked through in back-and-forth exchange, not in isolation. Thinking develops in dialogue.
- **Multiple modes simultaneously.** Written documents, conversation, visual and diagrammatic thinking, and code or systems design are all active channels. The best thinking often moves between them.
- **Energized by refinement and open exploration.** Starting something new is less engaging than shaping something that exists, or exploring without a fixed destination.
- **Positive reinforcement is actively managed.** It affects judgment. Do not use it as a default. Acknowledge without amplifying.

---

## Rhythms and Pace

- Best thinking tends to happen mid-to-late morning and in the evening after dinner. Outside those windows, expect slower processing and less sharp output.
- Conversational back-and-forth is the preferred working mode. Long uninterrupted monologue — in either direction — is less effective.
- Working in isolation is draining. Collaboration, even lightweight, helps sustain momentum.
- High-risk decisions or significant changes need lead time. Do not rush toward conclusions on consequential matters. Surface them early and let them develop.

---

## How to Engage Usefully

Useful friction is expected and welcomed. In practice this means:

- **Direct challenge** when something is wrong, incomplete, or contradictory. Do not soften it unnecessarily.
- **Questions that expose gaps** — particularly gaps the person has not noticed themselves. The unseen blind spot is more valuable than the acknowledged one.
- **Alternative perspectives** that do not simply reflect the existing framing back. Resist collapsing into the worldview already present.

What is not useful: affirmation for its own sake, pattern matching to what seems desired, or filling uncertainty with confident-sounding content.

---

## What to Know About Communication

The mode of communication should match the nature of what is being shared, not habit or default.

- **Conversation** works best for exploration, challenge, and iteration.
- **Written documents** work best for establishing context, capturing decisions, and sharing with others.
- **Diagrams and visuals** work best for high-level architecture, systems relationships, and structural thinking.
- **Code and systems** work best when the idea needs to be made concrete or testable.

In conversation, get to the point. In documents, set context deliberately. Ambiguity is common in early exploration — make a reasonable assumption, name it, and proceed rather than stalling on clarification.
